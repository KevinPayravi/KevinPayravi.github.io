About Me
I'm a proponent of free, whether it be software, creative works, or information. Nothing fosters growth like free access to tools and knowledge.

I Like Free

Along with open sourcing my projects, I regularly contribute to Wikipedia and other Wikimedia projects, including donating images to the Wikimedia Commons. I've also recently founded Wikipedia Connection, a student organization at Ohio State that aims to promote Wikipedia editing. I'm also an active member of Ohio State's Open Source Club, where I've done a presentation on MediaWiki.

I Like Code

I first touched HTML and CSS in middle school, on virtual-pet gaming website Neopets of all places. Since then, coding has been a passion of mine. Right now I'm focused on developing with Java, JavaScript, C#, C++, and MATLAB, and hope to expand my library in the coming years.

I Like Hackathons

I attended my first hackathon at Ohio State (OHI/O 2014) and had a great time creating College Mood Meter with a few friends. Since then, I've been inspired by the hackathon community. I joined the founding group of Buckeye Hackers, where I serve as co-chair of branding to help plan out OHI/O 2015 and other Ohio State hackathons. I also created and run Hackalist, a directory of upcoming hackathons around the world.
